# Top Financial KPIs — Ranked by Effectiveness

A complete executive‑grade KPI set covering profitability, cash flow, leverage, efficiency, returns, and solvency.

---

## 1. EBITDA Margin % — “Core operating profitability”

**Formula:** EBITDA ÷ Revenue

**What it measures:**  
How efficiently the business converts revenue into operating profit before financing and accounting noise.

**Interpretation:**  
- High margin: strong pricing power, cost control, operational efficiency  
- Low margin: weak competitive position, high operating costs  
- Falling margin: cost pressures or revenue quality issues  
- Rising margin: operational improvements or better product mix  

**Why it matters:**  
It cuts through accounting distortions and shows the true engine of the business.

---

## 2. NPAT Margin % — “What’s left after everything”

**Formula:** NPAT ÷ Revenue

**What it measures:**  
Bottom‑line profitability after tax and all expenses.

**Interpretation:**  
- High NPAT margin: strong business model, disciplined cost structure  
- Low NPAT margin: high interest, tax, depreciation, or weak pricing  
- Volatile NPAT margin: exposure to one‑offs or cyclical swings  

**Why it matters:**  
It tells you whether the business actually creates value for shareholders.

---

## 3. Operating Cash Flow (OCF) — “Cash from core operations”

**Formula:** Cash generated from operations

**What it measures:**  
The company’s ability to generate cash from day‑to‑day business activities.

**Interpretation:**  
- High OCF: strong working capital discipline  
- Low OCF: operational stress or poor cash conversion  
- Falling OCF: early warning sign of weakening fundamentals  

**Why it matters:**  
OCF is the foundation of liquidity, debt servicing, and FCF.

---

## 4. Free Cash Flow (FCF) — “Cash the business truly generates”

**Formula:** Operating Cash Flow – Net Capex

**What it measures:**  
How much cash is left after maintaining and growing the asset base.

**Interpretation:**  
- Positive FCF: business can fund itself  
- Negative FCF: heavy reinvestment or cash strain  
- Growing FCF: strong fundamentals  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s the KPI investors care about most.

---

## 5. Revenue Growth YoY % — “Top‑line momentum”

**Formula:** (Current Revenue – Prior Revenue) ÷ Prior Revenue

**What it measures:**  
The company’s ability to grow its customer base, pricing, and volume.

**Interpretation:**  
- High growth: strong demand or market share gains  
- Low growth: saturation or competitive pressure  
- Negative growth: structural decline  

**Why it matters:**  
Growth is the starting point for all valuation and margin analysis.

---

## 6. EBITDA Growth YoY % — “Operational momentum”

**Formula:** (Current EBITDA – Prior EBITDA) ÷ Prior EBITDA

**What it measures:**  
Whether operational performance is improving or deteriorating.

**Interpretation:**  
- High growth: strong execution  
- Low growth: margin pressure or cost inflation  
- Negative growth: operational deterioration  

**Why it matters:**  
It shows whether the business is scaling profitably.

---

## 7. Net Debt to EBITDA — “Debt sustainability”

**Formula:** Net Debt ÷ EBITDA

**What it measures:**  
The company’s ability to service its borrowings.

**Interpretation:**  
- < 2.0x: very safe  
- 2.0–3.0x: normal for capital‑intensive industries  
- > 3.5x: elevated risk  
- > 5.0x: distressed  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s the #1 leverage ratio used by banks and credit agencies.

---

## 8. Interest Coverage Ratio — “Ability to pay interest”

**Formula:** EBIT ÷ Net Interest Expense

**What it measures:**  
How comfortably the company can meet interest payments.

**Interpretation:**  
- > 5x: very strong  
- 2–5x: acceptable  
- < 2x: risk of financial stress  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s a leading indicator of solvency risk.

---

## 9. Capex to Revenue % — “Investment intensity”

**Formula:** Net Capex ÷ Revenue

**What it measures:**  
How much of revenue must be reinvested to sustain operations.

**Interpretation:**  
- High %: capital‑heavy business (mining, infrastructure)  
- Low %: asset‑light business (retail, software)  
- Rising %: expansion or maintenance catch‑up  
- Falling %: efficiency or underinvestment  

**Why it matters:**  
It reveals the true cost of running the business.

---

## 10. Asset Turnover — “Efficiency of asset use”

**Formula:** Revenue ÷ Total Assets

**What it measures:**  
How effectively the company uses its assets to generate revenue.

**Interpretation:**  
- High turnover: efficient, lean operations  
- Low turnover: heavy assets or underutilisation  
- Falling turnover: bloated balance sheet  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s a core driver of ROE and ROIC.

---

## 11. Return on Equity (ROE) — “Return to shareholders”

**Formula:** NPAT ÷ Equity

**What it measures:**  
How effectively the company generates profit from shareholder capital.

**Interpretation:**  
- High ROE: strong business model  
- Low ROE: weak profitability or overcapitalisation  
- Falling ROE: deteriorating economics  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s the ultimate measure of shareholder value creation.

---

## 12. Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) — “Return on all capital”

**Formula:** EBIT ÷ (Equity + Debt – Cash)

**What it measures:**  
How efficiently the company uses all capital — not just equity.

**Interpretation:**  
- High ROCE: strong competitive advantage  
- Low ROCE: poor capital allocation  
- Rising ROCE: improving economics  

**Why it matters:**  
It’s the best KPI for comparing businesses across industries.

---

## 13. Net Debt to Equity % — “Balance sheet leverage”

**Formula:** Net Debt ÷ Equity

**What it measures:**  
How much of the business is funded by debt vs shareholders.

**Interpretation:**  
- Low ratio: conservative  
- High ratio: aggressive capital structure  
- Rising ratio: debt‑funded growth or stress  

**Why it matters:**  
Useful, but less predictive than Net Debt/EBITDA.

---

## 14. Debt to Assets % — “Solvency snapshot”

**Formula:** Net Debt ÷ Total Assets

**What it measures:**  
How much of the asset base is financed by debt.

**Interpretation:**  
- Low %: strong solvency  
- High %: riskier balance sheet  

**Why it matters:**  
Good for solvency assessment, but less actionable than EBITDA‑based leverage.
